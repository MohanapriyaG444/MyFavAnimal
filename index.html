FUN FACT



The lion is the second largest member of the cat family. The lion’s scientific name is Panthera leo.

The lion has a long body, short legs, sharp claws, and a large head. Adult males are about 9 to 10 feet (2.7 to 3 meters) long, including the tail. They stand about 3 feet (1 meter) tall at the shoulder and can weigh 370 to 500 pounds (170 to 230 kilograms). Females are shorter and more slender.Lions are covered with yellow-brown hair. A male lion is easy to spot because of its mane. This coarse hair grows on its head, neck, and shoulders. Lionesses (females) do not have manes.

Facts About Lion

  • The lion is a carnivore and eats the flesh of other animals.
  • The lion is very good at hunting and hunts at night.
  • Lion is called as “King of Jungle” because of its huge size and hunting capacity.
  • The lion’s scientific name is Panthera leo.
  • The majority of the hunting in a group of lions is handled by the females.
  • The average lion will spend between 16 and 20 hours a day just resting or sleeping

Did you know...

  1. A lion's roar can be heard from 5 miles away.
  2. Lions can run at speeds of up to 50 miles per hour.
  3. African lions live in groups called ‘prides’.
  4. Lions run up to speeds of 80 Kph (50 Mph).
  5. They have an amazing jump, and have been recorded springing as far as 36 feet.
  6. The eyes of the lion are six times more sensitive to light than the eyes of a human
  7. The mane of the male lion gets darker as it ages so the darker mane of a lion in a pride denotes the oldest of the group.
